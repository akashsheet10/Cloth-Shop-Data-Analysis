#  Inventory Management & Sales Analysis Dashboard

## Overview

This project focuses on analyzing inventory performance and product sales using Excel. The dashboard helps identify dead stock, fast-moving products, slow-moving products, inventory turnover, and sell-through performance to support better inventory management decisions.

The analysis was performed on a retail inventory dataset containing product, supplier, stock, sales, and cost information.

---

## Project Objectives

- Monitor inventory levels across products.
- Identify dead stock products.
- Analyze fast-moving and slow-moving inventory.
- Calculate Sell-Through Percentage.
- Measure Inventory Turnover Ratio.
- Evaluate unsold inventory value.
- Support inventory replenishment and stock optimization decisions.

## Key Metrics Calculated

### Sell Through Rate

Measures how efficiently inventory is sold.

```text
Sell Through % = Stock Out / Stock In
```

### Inventory Turnover Ratio

Measures how frequently inventory is sold and replaced.

```text
Inventory Turnover = Sales / Average Inventory
```

### Unsold Inventory Value

```text
Unsold Value = Unsold Quantity × Unit Cost
```

---

## Dashboard Insights

### 📦 Dead Stock Analysis

Products with low sell-through rates and high unsold inventory were identified as dead stock.

Key Benefits:
- Reduce inventory holding costs
- Improve warehouse utilization
- Optimize procurement planning

---

### 🚀 Fast Moving Products

Products with high sales volume and strong sell-through rates were classified as fast-moving inventory.

Insights:
- High customer demand
- Frequent stock replenishment required
- Revenue-driving products

---

### 🐢 Slow Moving Products

Products showing low sales performance were identified as slow-moving inventory.

Insights:
- Lower turnover rates
- Higher inventory carrying costs
- Potential candidates for promotional offers
